What the Future Holds

About My Course

I am studying for a Diploma in Infocomm Security Management (DISM). It is a computing course focused on cybersecurity, learning ethical hacking and digital forensics.

  • Year 1 focuses on building computing fundamentals such as programming and learning computer parts. The first semester in Year 1 is the same for all School of Computing courses, but become more specialised toward the second semester.
  • In Year 2, the content is shifted toward what the course is known for, cybersecurity. It focuses mainly on hacking and the backside of systems, such as back-end web development.
  • Lastly, Year 3 is mostly spent on your Final Year Project (FYP) and in your internship. Thus, the content taught in Year 3 will be very minimal.

Potential Careers

The cybersecurity field has many career options; listed below are some of the available options.

  • Cybersecurity Specialist/Technician. Focuses on protecting systems from cybersecurity risks, threats, and vulnerabilities, through monitoring and detecting bugs.
  • Incident Analyst/Responder. Investigates cybersecurity or network incidents, diagnosing issues for each event.
  • IT Auditor. Centres around analysing a company or organisation's tech structure, providing valuable insight and possible improvements.

Career Prospect

With our world gravitating toward a digital future, the rate of cyberattacks will inevitably increase. As such, the demand for professional cybersecurity personnel has been growing and will continue to do so. Unfortunately, however, the supply of these professionals is insufficient. As such, every graduate in the cybersecurity field will be presented with many opportunities to grow themselves and their careers. Hence, being in the cybersecurity industry at this time is genuinely opens many possibilities.
